Micro Unit
Media: Balsa wood, cardboard, yellow tracing paper, chop board, x-acto blade, hot glue, sand paper, vellum, Faber Castell pen set, Rapidopen.
Project: Build a micro-unit housing at a sloped site at Cal Poly, following the area limit requirement, reduce amount of tree elimination, and use an insect as concept inspiration.
Detail: The insect selected was a snail. The idea of reducing tree elimination becomes an inspiration that the design would later centering on.
From there, several keywords was developed as study models created: curve, protection, going in and out, layer. From those keywords, concept that determines the final design were set.
The texture of tree and pattern of snail shell also influence the eventual decision of using a wood material for a curvature building.
Concave indicates: going in, entrance, exit.
Convex indicates: Protected, looking outside, safety, view.

San Francisco Crestmount D-House
The site is located in a high income district populated with college graduates from the nearby San Francisco State University. High up in the hills, the house would face the beautiful and serene scenery rarely found in a densely populated city like San Francisco. However, its location and limited lot size means the building must deal with the lack of light and heavy fog.
The open floor plan, wide span of windos, and exposed stairs was designed to expose the best views and eliminate visual obstruction. The materials define and represent the 2 units in the house. The lighter upper unit welcome its users with its warm and wooden materials, while the darker lower unit brightens the room with its airy acrylic service and glass-centric design. Together, the materials reflects the duo nature of the woody hill and translucent fog in the area.

USBGBC Natural Talent Competition entry: Home on the Bayou
Interested in working with an interdisciplinary team and green build, I joined the studio of Mary Margaret during fourth year. The class assignment ended up combining both - the aim was the USBGC Natural Talent Competition. I worked on the project with students from architecture, construction management, architectural engineering, and landscape architecture. The project site is in New Orleans, design requirements inspired by Hurricane Katrina. Some of the requirement is that the project should be completed on a limited budget, the building should be hurricane proof, and the living unit seven feet above ground. Attempt should be made to archive a LEED rating of Platinum.
Our house aimed to encourage interaction between users and architecture, individual and community, and human and environment. The site environment was carefully analysis, and the house was designed to be a place where human can control and improve with their very own hands. Some of the features includes movable planters, furnitures with hidden storage, and a open floor plan that allows future changes. Plans are designed to be open and universal access, but without sacrificing a sense of home for all.
